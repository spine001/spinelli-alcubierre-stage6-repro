# Stage 7E Gaussian Width Extension Summary

Stage 7E extended the N61 Gaussian pulse parameter sweep to wider smooth pulses.

Cases tested:

- geometry: gaussian_pulse
- N: 61
- amplitudes: 0.010 and 0.025
- widths: 1.25, 1.50, 1.75, 2.00

Main findings:

1. Action/Fit moves toward unity as the Gaussian pulse becomes wider.
2. beta_fit moves toward zero as the Gaussian pulse becomes wider.
3. The trend is similar for amplitudes 0.010 and 0.025, suggesting that Action/Fit is more width/geometry dependent than amplitude dependent.
4. The Bianchi diagnostic remains small and stable.
5. The Gaussian-pulse results support cross-geometry relevance of the action-derived correction tensor, but also show that beta=-1 is not universal outside steep wall-localized geometries.

Interpretation:

The Alcubierre beta=-1 result appears specific to steep wall geometries. In smooth Gaussian curvature pulses, the action-derived tensor remains useful, but the effective fitted beta is geometry- and scale-dependent.
