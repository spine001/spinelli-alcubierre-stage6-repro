# Stage 7G FRW Parameter Sweep Summary

Stage 7G tested the Spinelli correction framework on FRW-type cosmological expansion geometries.

Cases tested:

- geometry: frw
- N: 61
- amplitudes: 0.010, 0.025, 0.050
- omega: 1.0, 2.0, 3.0

Main findings:

1. Action/Fit remained very close to unity across all nine FRW cases.
2. Bianchi residuals remained small across the sweep.
3. beta_fit did not converge toward the Alcubierre steep-wall value near -1.
4. beta_fit appears geometry- and mode-dependent in FRW rather than universal.
5. The FRW results support cross-geometry relevance of the action-derived correction tensor, but require a cosmology-specific interpretation of beta.

Interpretation:

The action-derived correction tensor is residual-predictive in FRW-type expansion geometries. However, FRW behavior differs from the Alcubierre steep-wall and Gaussian-pulse cases. The emerging pattern is that the action tensor is robust, while fitted beta is geometry dependent.
